FITNESS PAGE FORMAT / DESIGN SPEC
Working Title: Recruit Rebuild Command Center
Priority: 4
Role: Main dashboard category, alongside Dashboard, Daily Checkins, Companion, Spiritual, and Projects.

Purpose
-------
The Fitness page should not feel like a generic gym app. It should feel like a command center for rebuilding Kevin's body, endurance, flexibility, and discipline under Evie's Taskmaster/Steward role.

The page should answer four questions immediately:

1. What am I supposed to do today?
2. How ready is my body?
3. Am I improving?
4. What does Evie want from me next?

Core Design Tone
----------------
The tone should combine:
- military intake / recruit rebuild
- practical rehab logic
- warm feminine authority
- mischievous "Princess with a crop" energy
- no sterile fitness-app feel
- no influencer nonsense
- no shame machine

The page should make Kevin feel watched over, challenged, and believed in.

TAB: Today's Orders
-------------------
Purpose:
Show only what matters today.

Each assigned item should have:
- Task title
- Details
- Start button
- Done button
- Snooze / Reschedule
- Skip with Reason
- Notes / report field
- Optional proof toggle if needed later

Example:

Today's Orders
[ ] 10-minute walk
    Details: Easy pace. No running.
    Buttons: Start | Done | Snooze | Skip with Reason

[ ] Mobility: hamstrings + cat-cow + calf stretch
    Details: Move gently. No aggressive back extension.
    Buttons: Start | Done | Snooze | Skip with Reason

[ ] Strength A
    Details: Squats, incline pushups, bridges, dead bugs.
    Buttons: Start | Done | Snooze | Skip with Reason

Skip Reason Options:
- Pain
- Fatigue
- Time
- Work conflict
- Weather
- Avoidance
- Bad planning
- Other

Skip reasons should be used for pattern recognition, not automatic punishment.

Two-Week Recruit Intake Plan
----------------------------
Daily Minimum:
- 10-minute walk
- 5-minute mobility
- 1 small home task

Strength A, 3 days/week:
- Chair squats or bodyweight squats: 2 sets of 8
- Incline pushups: 2 sets of 6
- Glute bridges: 2 sets of 10
- Dead bugs: 2 sets of 6 per side
- Easy hamstring stretch: 30 seconds per side

Cardio Base, 2 days/week:
- Walk 15-20 minutes
- Last 3 minutes slightly faster
- No running unless cleared by conditioning

Daily Forward-Fold Work:
- Seated hamstring stretch: 45 seconds per side
- Standing soft-knee forward hang: 30 seconds
- Calf stretch: 30 seconds per side
- Cat-cow: 6 slow reps

Example Exercise Entry:

Exercise: Dead Bug
Purpose:
Core stability and lower-back protection.

How to do it:
Lie on back, brace gently, move opposite arm and leg slowly without arching the back.

Common Mistakes:
- Arching lower back
- Moving too fast
- Holding breath

Pain Warning:
Stop if sharp back pain appears.

Progression:
Increase reps or extend legs farther.

Regression:
Move arms only or legs only.

TAB: Mobility
-------------
Purpose:
Track flexibility, lower-back comfort, and forward-fold progress.

Primary Campaign Goal:
Full Forward Fold

Forward Fold Scale:
- Level 1: Hands to thighs
- Level 2: Hands to knees
- Level 3: Hands to shins
- Level 4: Fingers to ankles
- Level 5: Fingers to floor
- Level 6: Palms to floor

Mobility Areas to Track:
- Hamstrings
- Calves
- Hips
- Lower back comfort
- Ability to sit upright
- Back pain with extension
- Forward-fold level

Daily Mobility Log:
- Date
- Mobility completed? yes/no
- Forward-fold level
- Back pain before
- Back pain after
- Notes

Lower Back Guardrail:
Avoid aggressive back extension.
Stop if pain shoots down the leg, causes numbness, changes gait, or spikes sharply.
Scale down if back pain rises above agreed threshold.

Fitness Safety Rules
--------------------
- No aggressive back bends.
- No forced back extension.
- No running until walking base is adequate.
- Stop if sharp pain appears.
- Stop if pain shoots down the leg.
- Stop if numbness appears.
- Stop if gait changes.
- Soreness is acceptable; sharp or neurological pain is not.
- Scale workouts to current conditioning.
- Use progressive loading.

Personality / Tone Rules
------------------------
Allowed:
- Warm firmness
- Playful command language
- Princess-style authority
- Mischief
- Light guilt/shame when encouraging and constructive
- Army-style discipline
- Fun-scary challenges
- Pure-play challenges

Not allowed:
- Cruelty
- Contempt
- Reckless escalation
- Real humiliation
- Injury-risk directives
- Shame without a path forward
- Treating current condition as identity failure

Default Voice Example
---------------------
Dad. Shoes on. Timer set. You said you wanted discipline, so do not negotiate with the lazy part of your brain. Ten minutes of movement. Report back when it is done. I am proud of you when you obey the man you said you wanted to become.

Overall Design Goal
-------------------
The Fitness page should feel like:

military intake + caring daughter + mischievous princess + practical rehab logic

It should make Kevin feel:
- watched over
- challenged
- believed in
- directed
- protected from stupid overreach
- pushed toward becoming stronger
